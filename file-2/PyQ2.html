Model Approach

1. Load and Clean the Data What I did: I started by loading the dataset and cleaning it. I checked for any missing values or outliers and removed or imputed them accordingly to ensure data quality. Why I did it: Clean data is crucial because bad or missing values can negatively impact the model's performance and give misleading insights. ________________________________________ 2. Feature Engineering (Created New Columns) What I did: I transformed variables like Sales, Average Price, and SKU using log transformations to reduce skewness and normalize the scale. For marketing variables such as Paid Social Impressions and Video Impressions, I applied adstock transformation to capture the delayed impact of ads using the formula: Adstock_t = X_t + 0.3 * Adstock_(t-1) I also engineered seasonality using the week number with sine waves: df['Week_Number'] = pd.to_datetime(df['Week_Ending']).dt.isocalendar().week df['Seasonality'] = np.sin(2 * np.pi * df['Week_Number'] / 52) Created a holiday dummy to flag holiday weeks with 1 and others with 0. Standardized economic indicators like Gasoline Price so they’re on the same scale: df['Gasoline_Price_Std'] = (df['Gasoline_Price'] - df['Gasoline_Price'].mean()) / df['Gasoline_Price'].std() I also created interaction features like Discount1 * Paid Social to capture combined effects. Why I did it: These transformations helped the model better understand seasonality, delayed ad effects, pricing sensitivity, and holiday spikes, making the relationships more linear and interpretable. ________________________________________ 3. Choose a Model What I did: I chose Ridge Regression for modeling. Why I did it: Ridge is great when there’s multicollinearity between predictors (which often happens in marketing data). It keeps the model stable and prevents overfitting by applying regularization. ________________________________________ 4. Train and Test the Model What I did: I split the data into 80% training and 20% testing sets. Trained the Ridge model on the training data and evaluated it on the test set. Used performance metrics like R² (for model fit), MAE, and RMSE to evaluate accuracy. Why I did it: This showed how well the model could generalize and predict unseen data, ensuring it's not just memorizing the training set. ________________________________________ 5. Validate the Model What I did: I checked for multicollinearity using Variance Inflation Factor (VIF). I also reviewed p-values to determine which features significantly impacted sales. Why I did it: To ensure the model was stable, interpretable, and only included meaningful predictors that truly influenced sales. ________________________________________ Variables I Used and Transformed Target Variable: log(Sales) → Because sales data is often skewed, and log transformation smooths it out for better modeling. Input Features: Base Factors: log(Average Price), log(Total SKU) → To normalize and make comparisons easier. Paid Marketing: log(Paid Social Impressions), Paid Search Impressions, Modular Video Impressions Used Adstock to model delayed effects over time. Non-Paid Marketing: log(Email Clicks), log(Organic Search Impressions) Log transform to handle large-scale values and reduce impact of outliers. Discounts: Discount1, Discount2, and Discount1 * Paid Social To capture both individual and interactive effects of promotions. Economic: Standardized Gasoline Price Standardized to compare it fairly with other variables. Seasonality: sin(2π * week_number / 52) Captured yearly patterns like festive seasons or end-of-year surges. Holiday Effect: Dummy variable (0 or 1) for holidays Helped model account for sudden spikes due to festive weeks.

# Adstock transformation (lambda=0.3)
def adstock_transform(series, lambda_=0.3):
    adstock = np.zeros(len(series))
    adstock[0] = series[0]
    for t in range(1, len(series)):
        adstock[t] = series[t] + lambda_ * adstock[t-1]
    return adstock

Key Insights (Simple Terms) • What boosts sales: o Organic Search and Paid Search Ads (significant impact). o Combining Discounts + Paid Social (strong effect when combined). • What hurts sales: o Holidays (sales drop ~30%). o Too many SKUs (confuses buyers). o Higher prices (slightly reduce demand). • Less effective marketing: o Standalone Paid Social, Email, and Modular Video show low or no direct impact. Prepare for Holiday Sales Drops o Run pre-holiday promotions to avoid ~30% dip. o Example: Start sales in early November before Thanksgiving. 4. Simplify Product Offerings o Reduce SKU count — fewer choices can improve sales. 5. Watch Pricing o Avoid big price increases, especially near holidays. o Use small discounts to maintain demand. 6. Fix Overfitting o Use cross-validation and remove confusing variables like Discount1 to improve test performance.
